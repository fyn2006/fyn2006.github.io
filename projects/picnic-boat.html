Picnic Table Motor Boat

A multi-year engineering project focused on designing and refining a functional picnic table motor boat capable of transporting passengers safely across water while maintaining stability, structural integrity, and propulsion efficiency.

Over the course of two years, multiple design iterations were created to solve structural, steering, flotation, and drivability issues identified during testing.

The second-generation design introduced major improvements in weight distribution, frame rigidity, propulsion mounting, and steering responsiveness.

This project consisted of four major stages:

  1. Initial concept development and structural frame design
  2. Construction and testing of the first-generation prototype
  3. Identifying design failures and performance limitations
  4. Developing and testing the improved second-generation model
YEAR 1 — FIRST GENERATION

Initial Prototype

The first version of the picnic table boat was designed around a lightweight welded frame and flotation system intended to support multiple passengers while maintaining maneuverability.

Problems Identified

Testing revealed several structural and operational problems, including poor balance, frame flexing under load, unstable steering, and excessive vibration from the motor mount.

Figure 1 — First-generation picnic table boat prototype
YEAR 2 — SECOND GENERATION

Redesigned Structure

The second-generation design focused on correcting the major issues discovered during testing of the original prototype.

Improvements Implemented

The redesigned model included a reinforced frame, improved weight distribution, upgraded steering geometry, and a more rigid propulsion mounting system to reduce vibration and improve handling.

Figure 2 — Second-generation picnic table boat redesign

Testing & Implementation

The improved design underwent additional testing to validate stability, passenger load capacity, steering performance, and propulsion reliability under real operating conditions.

Lessons learned from both generations of the project were used to refine fabrication methods and improve long-term durability.
